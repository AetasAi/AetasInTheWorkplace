The structure

Two short conversations. Both designed around your time.

The Workplace Performance Review takes place across two sessions — each around 30 minutes. Together they give a complete picture of what is happening in your business and what, if anything, is worth doing about it.

Conversation one
Discovery
Approximately 30 minutes — the session you have just booked

A structured diagnostic. We are not pitching — we are listening. The goal is to understand your organisation clearly before drawing any conclusions.

Where your business is now — your workforce, your current benefits and pension arrangements, and how they are structured
Where financial pressure may be showing up — in absence, turnover, or the management time your team is spending on people issues
What you already have in place, and whether it appears to be working as well as it should

This is an exploratory conversation, not a sales meeting. There is no agenda beyond understanding your situation clearly. Whether or not further work makes sense, you will leave with a clearer picture than you arrived with.

Conversation two
Findings and next steps
Approximately 30 minutes — typically within 10 days of the first conversation

The second session brings the picture back to you. We will have reviewed what you shared and identified where the most significant opportunities or concerns sit.

What the Review suggests about the cost of financial pressure in your specific business
Where the most significant opportunities for improvement are — in your pension, benefits, or people support arrangements
What a proportionate response would look like — and what it would cost
A written summary you can use with your leadership team, partners, or board

If further work makes sense for your organisation, we will say so and show you the numbers. If it does not, we will say that too. The conversation has no predetermined outcome.

Before we speak

You do not need to prepare. But these help.

The conversation flows more easily if you have a rough sense of the following. None of it needs to be precise — the point of the Review is to establish the picture, not for you to have already built it.

01
Your current headcount and a rough average salary range across your workforce
02
The pension provider you use, and roughly what employer contributions you currently pay into it
03
Any other benefits currently in place — healthcare, life cover, income protection, cycle to work, anything — even if you are not sure they are used
04
Whether you have seen any notable staff turnover in the past twelve months — even a rough sense of numbers

These are starting points, not homework. If you do not have them to hand, that is fine — part of what the Review surfaces is what you currently know and do not know about your own arrangements.
