The destination

You are not buying a system. You are buying a return.

Every employer invests heavily in people, salaries, pensions, benefits, recruitment, training, leadership. Return on Employee Investment (ROEI) is the measure of what that investment gives back in productivity, retention and commercial performance. Improving ROEI is the outcome employers actually want. The Aetas Performance System is how we deliver it.

The model

One system, on a single page

The same repeatable loop underpins every engagement. It starts with measurement, acts only on the causes it finds, and ends by proving the commercial return, all in service of a higher ROEI.

How it works

Three stages. One repeatable method.

The philosophy is not a slogan, it is the sequence. Each stage produces evidence for the next.

Stage 1 · Measure the loss

The Business Performance Review

Every engagement begins here. A structured diagnostic that quantifies, in pounds, where value is being lost, and sets your baseline ROEI. Free at the start of every engagement.

See engagement levels →

Stage 2 · Fix the cause

Improve financial wellbeing

We identify what is actually driving the loss, then deploy only the parts of the financial wellbeing programme that address that cause. No fixed menu, no predetermined recommendation; each part is introduced only where it earns its place.

Stage 3 · Prove the return

Measured ROEI improvement

We re-measure and show the commercial return against the original cost. The engagement is judged on the number it moved, because return is measured, not assumed.

How ROEI is measured →

The measurement engine

How we measure ROEI

ROEI is the framework that turns investment in people into a documented number. It is how the system measures the loss at Stage 1 and proves the return at Stage 3, the same metric, before and after.

We measure and improve engagement and focus, productivity and output, retention and recruitment cost, absence and presenteeism, benefits utilisation, and employer-brand strength, and express the change in commercial terms.
