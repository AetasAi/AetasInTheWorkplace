Understanding Aetas ITW

The Proposition

Aetas in the Workplace is a consultancy-led financial wellbeing programme designed specifically for SMEs and mid-sized organisations — businesses where every employee counts and a corporate, off-the-shelf approach simply does not translate.

We offer a holistic solution that covers all three dimensions of employee financial wellbeing: benefits and reward — reviewing and optimising what you already provide; financial education and guidance — practical workshops, one-to-one support, and access to regulated financial planning through Aetas Wealth; and wellbeing and incentive initiatives — targeted programmes that strengthen engagement, culture, and performance.

Rather than managing multiple providers independently, employers access the full breadth of expertise through a single relationship with Aetas, supported by the Aetas Collective — our curated network of specialist partners who deliver across each area of the programme.

EAPs and Aetas ITW are fundamentally different in what they set out to do — and in what they actually deliver.

An EAP is a reactive support service. It gives employees access to a helpline or counselling when something has gone wrong. Most EAPs include a financial guidance element, but it is typically generic, signposting-based, and rarely used. Uptake rates across the industry are low — often below 5% — and the impact on business performance is difficult to measure.

Aetas ITW is a proactive, consultancy-led programme. We start with the employer — understanding your workforce, your existing benefits, and where financial pressure is affecting performance — and then design a structured approach that addresses root causes rather than symptoms. Where an EAP reacts, ITW anticipates.

The other critical difference is regulated financial advice. Through Aetas Wealth, employees can access a qualified, FCA-regulated Independent Financial Adviser as a workplace benefit — something an EAP cannot provide. That level of individual support is what genuinely changes financial outcomes for employees.

Many of our clients retain their EAP alongside Aetas ITW. They serve different purposes. What ITW adds is strategy, depth, and measurable commercial impact.

Aetas ITW is designed for organisations with between 10 and 500 employees — the range where financial wellbeing has the greatest individual impact and where the absence of a structured approach carries the highest risk.

Larger organisations often have dedicated HR and reward teams, established benefit platforms, and the budget for enterprise-grade solutions. Smaller businesses frequently rely on a handful of key people whose financial confidence and stability directly determines the organisation's resilience.

Our programme is built for the businesses in between — where there is genuine complexity and genuine need, but where most existing market solutions are either too expensive, too generic, or designed for a corporate workforce that looks nothing like yours.

If you have fewer than 10 employees, we would still welcome a conversation — some of our most impactful work is with smaller professional services firms where individual financial planning for founders and directors is central to the engagement.

The Aetas Collective is our curated network of specialist partners — independent businesses who deliver specific elements of the ITW programme where their expertise adds the most value.

The Collective covers the full breadth of the programme, from physical wellbeing and coaching through to HR advisory, recruitment, technology, and sustainability-led engagement. Every partner is independently selected for their quality, relevance, and fit with the Aetas approach.

For employers, the Collective means access to a wide range of expert support through a single relationship — without the complexity and cost of managing multiple providers independently. Aetas coordinates, introduces, and oversees where relevant. You only engage the partners who are genuinely relevant to your workforce and priorities.

You can explore the full Collective directory at itw.aetaspartners.com/Aetas-collective.

The Business Case

ROEI & Financial Wellbeing

Financial stress is not a personal problem that stays at home when an employee walks through the door. The research is unambiguous: employees under financial pressure show measurably higher levels of absenteeism, lower productivity, reduced concentration, and significantly higher likelihood of looking for a new role.

For an SME, the numbers are stark. Replace one mid-level employee and you typically spend between 50% and 200% of their annual salary on recruitment, onboarding, and lost productivity during the transition. Multiply that across a team where financial pressure is unaddressed and the cost is not a wellbeing issue — it is a commercial one.

Organisations that invest in financial wellbeing consistently report improvements in retention, engagement, and productivity. The return is measurable. The cost of doing nothing is equally measurable — most businesses simply have not yet connected the numbers.

Financial wellbeing also has a direct bearing on how your organisation is perceived as an employer. In a competitive labour market, a genuine, substantive approach to employee financial health is a differentiator — one that attracts people who take their careers seriously and stay with organisations that take them seriously in return.

Return on Employee Investment — ROEI — is the commercial return your organisation generates from its total people cost. Most businesses know their salary bill. Few measure what that investment actually delivers in terms of output, engagement, and performance. That gap is where Aetas ITW works.

We approach ROEI across three levers:

  • Reducing the cost of poor financial wellbeing — quantifying and addressing the cost of absenteeism, presenteeism, and turnover that is driven by financial stress. For most organisations, this cost is significantly higher than the cost of addressing it.
  • Improving the effectiveness of existing benefits spend — many organisations spend considerably on benefits that employees either do not understand or do not value. A structured benefits review typically uncovers significant scope to redeploy budget more effectively.
  • Building the commercial case for continued investment — through our Blueprint and Partnership stages, we help you track the metrics that connect people investment to business performance, so that financial wellbeing sits alongside revenue, margin, and retention as a reported business indicator.

The goal is not simply a happier workforce — though that follows. The goal is a business that performs better because its people are financially confident, engaged, and present.

We establish measurable baselines at the start of every engagement and track progress against them throughout. The specific metrics depend on your organisation and priorities, but typically include:

  • Employee retention rates and the cost of turnover
  • Absence rates and associated productivity loss
  • Benefits engagement and utilisation levels
  • Employee financial confidence, measured through periodic surveys
  • Pension engagement and contribution rates
  • Workshop attendance and feedback scores

In our Partnership stage, performance reporting is a standard deliverable — presented in a format that is meaningful to leadership rather than to an HR team. We believe the business case for financial wellbeing investment should be as rigorous as any other commercial decision.

What Employees Experience

Employee Experience

For employees, the experience is practical, accessible, and entirely voluntary. We do not run generic financial literacy sessions that feel like compliance training. Everything is designed for the realities of working life — relevant, clear, and delivered in a way that respects that your team's time is limited.

Core employee touchpoints typically include:

  • Financial wellbeing workshops — covering areas such as financial resilience, budgeting in a cost-of-living environment, pension fundamentals, and protection planning. Run in-person or remotely, at a time that works for your team.
  • One-to-one guidance sessions — employees can request individual conversations with our team for guidance on their personal financial position. These are signposting conversations, not regulated advice.
  • Access to a regulated financial adviser — through Aetas Wealth, employees can access an FCA-regulated Independent Financial Adviser for personal financial planning. This is arranged directly with the employee at no cost to the employer.
  • Specialist support through the Collective — where relevant, employees may be introduced to Collective partners covering areas such as physical wellbeing, resilience coaching, or legacy planning.

The tone throughout is warm, non-judgmental, and genuinely useful. We are not here to tell people what to do with their money. We are here to give them the confidence and the access to make better decisions for themselves.

The employer-facing programme — including workshops, benefits review, and consultancy — is delivered by Aetas Partners Limited and does not constitute regulated financial advice.

Where individual employees wish to access personal financial planning — on their pension, savings, investments, or protection — this is provided through Aetas Wealth, a trading style of Insight Financial Associates Limited. Aetas Wealth is authorised and regulated by the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA registration 458421) and operates under the full framework of FCA conduct rules.

For employers, this structure is straightforward and risk-free. You are not responsible for the advice given to individual employees. Aetas Wealth has its own client agreements, disclosure obligations, and professional indemnity arrangements. Your role is simply to make the access available — Aetas manages everything else.

Access to a regulated IFA as a workplace benefit is one of the most valued and distinctive elements of Aetas ITW. Most employers — including those with sophisticated benefits packages — do not currently offer it.
